Daimler AG Issues Blockchain Corporate Bond, Realizing Automatic Bond Transaction Cycle

“As part of a pilot trial, Daimler AG, the German automaker and the parent company of Mercedes-Benz has issued a corporate bond worth 100 million Euros.”

This news was officially announced today. The one-year corporate bond named Schuldschein is the result of a test bed jointly built up by Daimler AG and Germany’s biggest state-backed wholesale banks group.

Also participating in the project are three savings banks in Esslingen-Nürtingen, Ludwigsburg, and Ostalb. They played the role of lenders in the test withLandesbank Baden-Württemberg (LBBW) in this prototype system.

Daimler AG says that the whole transaction process from origination, distribution, allocation and execution of loan agreement to the confirmation of repayment and interest payments was automatic and digitalized through blockchain network. The technical support for lending was provided by the IT subsidiaries of Daimler AG and LBBW. The cryptographic signature was adopted to avoid manipulation.

Months before the execution of the pilot project, CoinDesk reported that Daimler linked up with Hyperledger block chain project led by Linux Foundation which according to previous reports aimed to explore on a more extensive manner the potential application of blockchain technology.

Daimler AG’s Chief Information Officer Jan Brecht said“we see blockchain as a promising technology, not fully mature yet but continuously growing. Now is the right time to get into it, build up knowledge, and form a network of like-minded people to share experiences”.

Besides Daimler AG, other automakers are also exploring the blockchain technology such as Porsche and Toyota. The latter has joined R3 blockchain alliance.
