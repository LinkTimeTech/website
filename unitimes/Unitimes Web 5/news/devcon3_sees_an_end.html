DEVCON 3: Ethereum Founder Proposed ‘Modest Proposal' for Future Challenges

“As Ethereum entered Metropolis stages, privacy, safety and scalability are the three main challenges that we need to deal with---Vitalik”

From November 1st to November 4th, members of the core researcher team, developer team, and management team of Ethereum were invited to the third Ethereum Developers Conference. The conference attracted attendees from a wide array of industries, including general developers, managers, entrepreneurs, investors, and students from all over the world. The conference was held at Cancun, Mexico.

This year’s conference saw more than 1,800 attendees, with 170 speakers announcing the latest developments in their respective research and development efforts. Compared to 2016, this represents an increase of 50% in the number of attendees and 150% in the number of speakers. Tickets to Devcon 3 were sold out 4 months before the conference, indicating the massive global impact of Ethereum.

The objective of the conference is to showcase the latest research achievements in blockchain and cryptocurrency. The 4-day schedule saw attendees participating in main conferences and breakout conferences, discussing decentralization utilities in finance, law, entertainment, supply chain and other industries. Nearly 10 P2P applications were also introduced in the breakout conference.

The founder of Ethereum, Vitalik Buterin showcased his “Modest Proposal', showing his future vision of Ethereum.

The proposal showed that Ethereum has now successfully entered the stage of metropolis with over 1,000 applications on the platform, where daily transactions reach up to 500,000. This translates to 7 transactions happening in every second. Additionally, Ethereum now has over 20,000 nodes worldwide. (where one was even found in North Korea!)

Vitalik demonstrated that privacy, safety and scalability are the currently the most salient challenges faced by cryptocurrency developers. To overcome these challenges, Vitalik mentioned sharding as a viable solution. Ethereum comprises of different shards, where some are main shards and others small shards. The main shards make up today's Ethereum network, allowing conservative and slower changes; whereas the smaller shards allow for more aggressive and abrupt changes. These small shards are called universes. This is how Ethereum maintains its stability, and motivates developers to maximize their innovation on the platform.

In addition, Vitalik announced the addition of a validator manager contract (VMC) in the main chain as the connection between different shards, to motivate PoS to ensure maximum security in all transactions.

He said that these changes will not affect ‘address space’ but instead create new ‘address spaces’ to increase the efficiency of the transactions at the same time.

In conclusion, all efforts from participants during the conference has contributed to making Ethereum better and more resilient to future challenges.
