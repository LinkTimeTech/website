China Bans all Token Offering Financing Activities, Price of the Virtual Currencies Decline

Editor: Xue Xu

4th Sept, 2017

On Sept. 4th, 2017, seven government administrations including the People’s Bank of China, China Securities Regulatory Commission, China Banking Regulatory Commission and China Insurance Regulatory Commission have all come together to issue a joint statement, echoing the committee’s stance on how ICOs are a form of unauthorized illegal fund raising.

According to the Statement, all organizations and individuals are now prohibited from the raising funds through any ICO activities. From the date of the Statement (Sep. 4), the organizations and individuals who have completed the token offering and token financing should make arrangements for refunding and managing the risks properly. Relevant authorities will crack down on the ongoing token offering and token financing activities which refuse to do so.

The Statement defines ICO as a practice of unauthorized fundraising which may involve illegal sale of tokens, illegal issuance of securities, illegal fund-raising, financial fraud, pyramid schemes and other criminal activities. The Statement said that the tokens or “virtual currencies” used in the ICOs are not issued by the authorized organizations, therefore they do not have the same legal status as the fiat currency, so they cannot and should not be circulated and used as the currency in the market.

ICO financing activities have emerged in large numbers this year and were misused by ill-intentioned speculators. Some ICO projects are highly suspected of financial scam, which has disrupted the economic and financial order. Recently, people are waiting for the regulatory policy for ICO and there were different speculations from the outside until the policy was formally released. In the last few days, the reports on ICOs have caused a stir in the bitcoin and blockchain field. The most striking and direct result is the sharp decline of the prices of the digital currencies.

Following the issuance of the statement, many investors said, “it’s time to sell out all my digital assets!!”. Some on-going projects announced that they would exit the market according to the national laws and regulations, and some oversea projects have also officially prohibited Chinese to participate. The prices of the virtual currencies have fluctuated wildly since then.
