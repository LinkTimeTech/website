IBM and Sony to Develop Educational Platform with Blockchain

“Securing and sharing student records, Blockchain begins to be used in Education .”

Sony Global Education plans to launch the educational platform using blockchain. The underlying technology of the platform is the IBM Blockchain officially launched in March.

The platform will allow educators to exchange information on student progress and achievements. According to the announcement, Sony is trying to eye both primary and higher educational institutions as users of the new product. Going forward, Sony said that it will develop additional educational services based on the blockchain platform used as the data-sharing layer.

Masaaki Isozu, president of Sony Global Education, said in a statement, “Blockchain technology has the potential to impact systems in a wide variety of industries, and the educational sphere is no exception when educational data is securely stored on the blockchain and shared among permissioned users.”

Sony also said that it is now observing the applications of the blockchain in the areas of supply chain and logistics and will continue to evaluate this system as it devotes to creating appealing products and services in different fields.
