Hong Kong SFC: ICO Tokens “May be Securities” & Subject to Laws

Editor: Xue Xu

5th Sept, 2017

On September 5, 2017, Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) released the Statement on Initial Coin Offerings, announcing that certain ICO tokens might be securities as defined in the Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO), and subjected to the securities laws of Hong Kong. And parties engaging in a “regulated activity” are required to be licensed by or registered with the SFC irrespective of whether the parties involved are located in Hong Kong, so long as such business activities target the Hong Kong public.

Just the day before, seven government departments in Chinese mainland jointly issued a Statement on the ban of ICO activities, calling for the immediate halt of all ICO tokens within the country.
