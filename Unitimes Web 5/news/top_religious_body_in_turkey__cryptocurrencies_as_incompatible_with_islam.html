Top religious body in Turkey denounces cryptocurrencies as incompatible with Islam

“Reasons include the volatility of cryptocurrencies, the lack of a central authority, and possible uses for money laundering”

December 02, 2017 – The Directorate of Religious Affairs, or Diyanet in Turkey has jointly announced in a statement that the use of cryptocurrencies is not compatible with Islam. Diyanet is the top religious body in Turkey. According to Diyanet, reasons for the incompatibility include the volatility, lack of central authority, and the possibility for money laundering activities facilitated by cryptocurrencies.

The statement, translated by Euronews, reads as follows:

“Buying and selling virtual currencies is not compatible with religion at this time because of the fact that their valuation is open to speculation, they can be easily used in illegal activities like money laundering and they are not under the state’s audit and surveillance.”

End of cryptocurrencies in Turkey?

The answer is a clear no. The statement by Diyanet is a stark contrast to sentiments towards cryptocurrencies by governmental bodies in Turkey. Early in November, The Central Bank of Turkey (CBRT) expressed enthusiasm and openness towards the future of virtual currencies in the country.

President of CBRT Murat Cetinkaya said virtual currencies can contribute to financial stability in Turkey, and the bank is researching possible use of cryptocurrencies with a positive attitude. A research group headed by CBRT is also formed, where participants include government officials and regulators.
