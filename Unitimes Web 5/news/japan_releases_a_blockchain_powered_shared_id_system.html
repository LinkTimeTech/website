Japan Releases A Blockchain-Powered Shared Id System

“People don’t need to reenter personal information with the shared ID system when opening accounts at new financial institutions, which helps people save much time and energy.”

The Financial Services Agency (FSA), one of the main regulatory and supervisory authorities for banks in Japan, is developing a blockchain-backed digital information sharing plan. As it explains, the plan will enable users to open accounts immediately at other banks via the shared ID system, and the banks will be allowed to share customers’ information through this platform to enhance the efficiency of business processing at different banks.

It is reported that users will be able to leverage the shared ID system to open accounts at other banks. The applicant will be asked to provide a shared ID and confirm his identity by means of a smartphone App via a fingerprint or a facial scanning. And afterwards the shared ID will be used to start a new account in another bank. To put it simply, when people open accounts at new financial institutions, with the shared ID system, they don’t need to reenter personal information.

According to the local report, when customers open accounts in a bank, they are required to verify their identities via mail. This process may take one week to complete. In contrast, customers can save more time and energy when they start accounts at banks leveraging the shared ID system.

The FSA said it will start a large-scale test for the performance of the system before the end of this year. If the test achieves success, the Bank of Tokyo-Mitsubishi UFJ (MUFJ), the Smitomo Mitsui Banking Corp and Mizuho, Japan’s three megabanks, will be the first institutions to launch the service.
