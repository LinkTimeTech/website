Japanese Internet Giant GMO Introduces Bitcoin Salary Payment System

"Japanese Internet giant GMO has launched a new payroll system to allow its 4,700+ staffs to receive part of their salaries in bitcoin.”

As Japanese largest internet service and online securities trading company, GMO recently announced that part of its people’s salary will be paid in bitcoin from February next year. GMO Group owns a total of 42 subsidiaries, including GMO coins, focusing on crytocurrency business, and GMO click, one of the world's largest FX platforms.

The company said in the statement that initially, this bitcoin payroll system will only be available to the staffs of GMO Internet Co. Ltd, but finally it will extend to the entire group. Acoording to the data from its official website, as of September this year, the number of full-time staffs of GMO Internet Co. Ltd has already reached 4,710.

Each salary payment in yen will be reduced by the amount of bitcoin payment paid, using the exchange rate at the GMO Coins exchange. The bitcoin salary that the staffs receive is about 10,000 yen to 100,000 yen (nearly $88 to $881). Those staffs who has created an account at the GMO Coins exchange can receive bitcoin on the payday.

In Monday’s announcement, the company stated that The GMO Internet Group will contribute to the development of virtual currencies in the world by promoting efforts related to virtual currency throughout the group. It is important for our staffs to positively use virtual currency and learn more about the related knowledge. In addition, the company will carry out mining business in January 2018.

Following the launch of the cryptocurrency trading platform in May, GMO exposed its mining plans in September. In the next few years, the company plans to build a mining farm and to develop 7nm, 5nm and 3.5nm mining chips.
