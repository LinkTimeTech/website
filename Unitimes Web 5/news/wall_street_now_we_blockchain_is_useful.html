Wall Street: "Now We Know Blockchain is Useful!"

"Blockchain can dramatically reduce back-end operation costs and speed up the pace of transaction settlement. Banks must have funds in reserve while waiting for deals to close, so billions of dollars can be released and used elsewhere once the transaction time lasted for days or weeks is reduced to minutes. "

Banks such as Goldman Sachs Group Inc. and JPMorgan Chase & Co. conducted blockchain technology tests using Axoni's AxCore blockchain software in the equity swap market valued $ 2.8 trillion, which took 6 months. The tests result show that blockchain can release a large amount of funds and increase the efficiency of their utilization. The application of the technology in financial service industry is closer to be a reality.

Greg Schvey, Axoni's CEO, said: "Now we know it works and we are moving it forward!"

The project, managed by Axoni, a blockchain start-up, is mainly to track swap contracts, record deals, split stock rights as well as adjust and terminate dividends, which ends with a success rate of 100%. Other participants include Citigroup Inc., Canada Pension Plan Investment Board, BNP Paribas SA and Credit Suisse Group AG.

Why blockchain can draw the attention of Wall Street?

The reason why blockchain can draw the attention of Wall Street is that it can significantly reduce the back-end operation costs and speed up the pace of transaction settlement. Banks must have funds in reserve while waiting for the transaction settlement, so billions of dollars can be released and used elsewhere once the transaction time lasted for days or weeks are reduced to minutes.

Adam Herrmann, Citi’s global head of prime finance, commented: "There are fewer valuation disputes and fewer check-ups, and real-time access to data is good for everyone in the industry."

The blockchain system of equity swap can speed up transaction time,because banks and asset managers can all become members of the network and share distributed ledgers. Each member has an up-to-date copy of the ledger, so the payment by one party to the other one can be processed almost instantly.

Application of smart contract technology

Axoni's AxCore blockchain software does not handle real transactions and has no cash flow, since the project is done in a test environment. The whole plan is to launch existing systems such as Fedwire or Swift if the project comes into operation. AxCore blockchain software adopts Ethereum's smart contract technology, with only minor changes.

"It's much like Ethereum, but still very different from Ethereum, for example, it’s better in its scalability, privacy, and security," said Schvey.
