Nokia Looks to Blockchain Technology for Users to Share Health Data Securely

“Nokia is partnering with OP Financial Group to launch a blockchain pilot program, aimed at boosting customer confidence in sharing health data securely”

Nokia has announced via a blogpost that it is launching a blockchain pilot program with OP Financial Group that will see users share their health data via blockchain technology. According to Nokia, users are presently wary of sharing their health data with due to privacy concerns.

This pilot program aims to build an eventual blockchain platform that users will find trustworthy to share their personal health data from Nokia wearable devices. As written in the blog post:

“Nokia’s experimental blockchain platform used in the pilot puts the focus on privacy and giving users full control over their data.”

Accordingly, blockchain technology will encrypt all shared data, and the data can only be read by the intended recipient. This also means that Nokia and OP Financial Group will be unable to decipher any health data sent via blockchain. All they can see are records of (1) decision of a user to share data and (2) roof of authenticity of the data coming from a valid device.

Kristian Luoma, Head of OP Lab at OP Financial Group, shared that blockchain technology can be creatively applied to boost consumer trust in a variety of industries.

“We’re excited about the collaboration with Nokia. It is great to see Blockchain applied to something that needs trust, like in this our pilot we’re joining forces. This pilot is quite literally a prime example of the technologies we’ll use in the future, combined innovatively to create value,” she said.
