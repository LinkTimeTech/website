Samsung SDS Cooperates with Seoul Government for Blockchain Platform

“Samsung SDS has signed a deal with the Seoul Metropolitan Government to jointly develop its "information strategy" plan. It has been nearly seven months since Samsung unveiled Nexledger – an enterprise-grade blockchain platform hosted in the cloud”

According to the local media Korea Herald  , Samsung SDS has signed a deal with the Seoul Metropolitan Government to jointly develop its "information strategy" plan, that is, using blockchain technology to support government’s administration in terms of welfare, public safety and transportation affairs. This move aims at building a blockchain platform for public sector.

It is also reported that the Seoul government hopes to enhance the transparency of its administration and make it easier for the public to access to blockchain technology. Regarding to this cooperation, CEO of Samsung SDS stated that Samsung SDS’ blockchain technology and consulting capabilities will be a great contributor to Seoul’s leap as a top-rate global city.

In order to improve the company’s competitiveness and promote business development, in fact, Samsung SDS has been involved in blockchain space in different ways, for instance, in June 2016, the company has invested in Blocko, a Korea-based blockchain startup, mainly to make the company’s information and communication technologies (ICT) product portfolio more diversified; in April 2017, the company announced Nexledger, an enterprise-grade blockchain platform hosted in the cloud.

Besides, Samsung SDS, the IT subsidiary of the South Korean electronics giant with an annual income over 1 billion dollars, announced this June the building of blockchain alliance with government agencies and logistics enterprises in the country to upgrade the logistics process. Among which, Korea Customs Service, Ministry of Oceans and Fisheries, Hyundai Merchant Marine and IBM Korea are one of the members of the alliance.
