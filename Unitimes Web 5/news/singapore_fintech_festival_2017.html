Singapore Fintech Festival 2017: Singapore, Hong Kong and Canada Will Use Blockchain for Trade Finance; MAS and ABS Publicly Release Source Codes

“MAS will work with Hong Kong’s Monetary Authority and Bank of Canada to develop blockchain payments; MAS and ABS releases source codes of prototypes under Project Ubin”

Singapore launched its second Fintech Festival on 13 November 2017, a week-long event hosting 25,000 participants from 100 countries. Dubbed the world’s largest fintech festival, the program includes talks from Fintech experts and networking sessions for startups and businesses.

The festival is organized by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Partners include The Association of Banks (ABS) and SingEx Holdings.

Blockchain was a huge topic during the Singapore Fintech Festival 2017 (SFF 2017). We attended the event, and here are the latest developments in blockchain announced on Day 1 and Day 2:

1.MAS will work with Hong Kong and Canada to develop a trade finance platform powered by blockchain technology

In our article yesterday, we reported that James Henry Lau, Secretary for Financial Services and the Treasury in Hong Kong said that blockchain could provide “great benefits” to trade finance between countries.

During SFF 2017, MAS announced that it is working with Hong Kong’s Monetary Authority and Bank of Canada to build a cross border trade finance platform with blockchain technology.

MAS managing director Ravi Menon said that “blockchain technology offers good promise to make trade finance safer and more efficient.”

“Today, trade finance is largely paper-based. This is not only inefficient, but increases risks such as fraud and duplicate invoicing,” he added.

2.MAS and ABS released source codes for blockchain prototypes to the public

MAS and ABS have released source codes for blockchain prototypes to the public under the Project Ubin initiative. The Project Ubin initiative was launched in November 2016 by a consortium of banks and technology companies led by the MAS. Its goal is to simplify and provide more security for clearing and settlement of payments and securities.

Project Ubin has gone through two phases so far. The first phase concluded in 9 March 2017, where a report was produced by Deloitte that covers aspects of distributed ledger technology that are most suited to settlement systems and details the design principles used for the prototype.

The second phase saw MAS and ABS successfully develop “software prototypes of three different models for decentralised inter-bank payment and settlements with liquidity savings mechanisms.”

To conclude the second phase, MAS and ABS have now publicly released source codes of the three blockchain prototypes. In a statement, they said that “Central banks, financial institutions, as well as academic and research institutions will be able to tap the publicly-released source codes to facilitate their experiments, research and innovation.”

MAS chief fintech officer Sopnendu Mohanty also said that "Open-sourcing these prototypes will encourage other central banks to conduct similar trials and engender further development on domestic inter-bank payments."

"We aim to link up such DLT-based systems in the future for improved cross-border connectivity. This is our contribution to the global financial ecosystem, and a step towards developing cheaper, safer and more efficient cross-border payments,” he added.
