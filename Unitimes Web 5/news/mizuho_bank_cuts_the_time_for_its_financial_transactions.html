Mizuho Bank Cuts the Time for Its Financial Transactions with Australia from Days to 2 Hours through Blockchain

“A trade finance transaction through blockchain ended between Mizuho Bank, a Japanese banking giant and Australia. The detailed information of this trade was also released. ”

Mizuho Bank, one of Japan’s leading three banks has already finished a trade transaction in blockchain trial, involving the process of exporting goods from Japan to an Australian importer. According to details unrolled by the bank’s FinTech Department, all processes from the issuance of the letter of credit to the delivering of trade documents take place on the digital blockchain platform.

Participants of this trade also include the insurer Sompo Japan Nipponkoa Insurance and the Marubeni Corporation. Reaching a conclusion, Moziho Bank underlined the benefits and hurdles of having blockchain-backed trade finance transactions.

The time spent in delivering trade documents was shortened to 2 hours from multiple days in the test, which is a distinctive edge of blockchain. The labor cost was reduced by digitalizing the documents. The function of providing a real-time snapshot of a trade for participants and improving transparency is considered another advantage.

The drawback revealed from the test is that the trade information was not accessible to participants who haven’t adopted or used blockchain.

The Moziho Bank remarked “Building on this trade transaction project, Mizuho aims to further explore the practical business application of blockchain/DLT and to offer technologically sophisticated, client-focused services going forward,”

Like other Japanese banks, Muziho also tested blockchain solutions. In Feburary 2016, it finished a 3-month trial on the securities transactions through blockchain. The bank claimed that it was“practically impossible to tamper with transaction histories”. The cross-border securities can be settled the same day instead of three days later.

As an investor of Japan’s biggest Bitcoin exchange known as Bitflyer, Muziho finished its digital currency test early this year. In the middle of 2016, the bank and IBM in cooperated developing a virtual currency. In December, 2016, the bank made a real-world test which was paying for a group eat-together with its own digital currency.
