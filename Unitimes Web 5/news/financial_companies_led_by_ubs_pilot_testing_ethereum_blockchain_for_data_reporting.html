Financial Companies Led by UBS Pilot Testing Ethereum Blockchain for Data Reporting

“The Ethereum platform can provide the transparency needed to fulfil new regulations on data sharing set by the European Union”

Swiss bank UBS is leading a consortium of financial firms to pilot test an Ethereum platform for data reporting, in a bid to fulfil new compliance standards set by the European Union (EU). Participants in this testing stage include Barclays Bank, Credit Suisse, KBC, Swiss stock exchange SIX and Thomson Reuters.

The Markets in Financial Instruments Directive II set by the EU will come into force Jan 3, 2018, which requires financial firms to reporting trade data, to prevent market abuse. The Ethereum platform is reported to provide the transparency required for this task, as data cannot be tampered under a distributed ledger network.

“The project is getting market participants to collaborate using blockchain to improve regulatory reporting,” said Peter Stephens, head of blockchain at UBS, in an interview with Reuters.

“The point is to allow us to come to a consensus for this noncompetitive reference data which is essential for trade reporting purposes,” Stephens added.

The blockchain will be hosted on the Microsoft Azure cloud, allowing all participating firms to cross reference their own data with other firms on the Ethereum network. The pilot test is said to be able to detect anomalies between different sets of submitted data, alerting all concerned parties and calling for a resolution.

Ethereum is a decentralized platform that allows users to build applications using blockchain technology, for a variety of uses such as creating markets, storing registries of debt or move funds. It’s native cryptocurrency Ether has, at the time of writing, hit an all-time high of USD635, achieving a market cap of almost USD 61 billion dollars.

Ether’s current rise represents almost a rise of 50% compared to last week, where the price was hovering around USD400. It is the second largest cryptocurrency in the world after bitcoin, in terms of market cap and price.
