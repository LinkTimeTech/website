Australia Is World’s First to Replace Existing Technology for Trade Platform with Blockchain

“With Digital Asset as partner, the Australian Securities Exchange aims to release the first blockchain prototype by March 2018 for market review”

Australian Securities Exchange (ASX) has announced via a press release its confirmation to use blockchain technology to facilitate trades on its platform. This is the first instance where a country’s securities exchange organization is confirmed to be powered by blockchain.

Over the past two years, ASX has been partnering with Digital Asset (DA) to incorporate distributed ledger technology (DLT) into its operations. ASX now plans to work on finalizing the scope Of Day 1 functionality for the new system and releasing it for market feedback by end of March 2018.

The new blockchain powered platform replaces the incumbent CHESS (Clearing House Electronic Subregister System) system, used by ASX to “record shareholdings and manage the clearing and settlement of equity transactions in Australia”. Although ASX claims that CHESS is still a robust and reliable system, blockchain is now chosen for its enhanced functionality, security and resilience.

“ASX has been carefully examining distributed ledger technology for almost two-and-a-half years, including the last two years with Digital Asset, in order to understand its potential application. Having completed this work, we believe that using DLT to replace CHESS will enable our customers to develop new services and reduce their costs, and it will put Australia at the forefront of innovation in financial markets,” said Dominic Stevens, ASX Managing Director and CEO.

The new system will reportedly differ from traditional blockchain platforms, in that it will only allow copies of shared ledgers to be stored only on a secure private network consisting of verified participants. Conversely, traditional blockchain systems are completely decentralized – meaning that shared ledgers and transactional records are stored on individual computers all around the world, by participants who are unknown and anonymous.
