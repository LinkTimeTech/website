Air New Zealand Works with Winding Tree to Explore Applications of Blockchain Technology in Aviation Sector

“Aviation industry is a data-intensive industry with complex procedures, which can gain a lot from blockchain technology.”

Air New Zealand has recently announced that it will team up with Switzerland-based decentralized travel platform Winding Tree to explore applications of blockchain technology in aviation sector. According to the report, their collaboration will look into whether the technology can help the national flag carrier airline of New Zealand improve the security and efficiency of services including baggage tracking and ticket booking.

One of the benefits that blockchain technology has brought to the aviation industry, especially to the consumers, is that people may get more information about flight data and the cases of asymmetric flight information between airlines and consumers will be relatively reduced. Furthermore, blockchain technology can also better promote the cooperation between airlines, airports, IT suppliers and ground crew.

An airline’s multiple information processing systems need to handle a large amount of information, ranging from passenger services to personnel management. For airline companies, the system integrity and revenue will face great challenges once problems occur, while the use of blockchain technology can help enhance data processing efficiency and share data information among different nodes.

Air New Zealand has also revealed that it will invest in Winding Tree’s ICO project, which is planned to be launched in January 2018. In October this year, Winding Tree has announced a partnership with Germany’s Lufthansa with the aim to develop blockchain-based travel marketplace supported by a cryptographic token.
