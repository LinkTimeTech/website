Hewlett Packard Enterprise Is to Launch Blockchain-based New Product in 2018

“Enterprises interested in blockchain are now realizing that public cloud alone does not always meet their non-functional requirements”

Business technology firm Hewlett Packard Enterprise(HPE) plans to launch a new Blockchain-based service next year.

HPE says it will use R3’s Corda platform, a distributed ledger startup, as the underlying basis to provide services around payment, identity and others. These two firms publicly announced their partnership this June.

The “Mission Critical Distributed Ledger Technology” is the first of it’s series of products that use blockchain, which is also being deployed to enterprises as the extension of cloud-based services. The firm’s blockchain director, Raphael Davison indicated in a statement that Enterprises interested in blockchain are now realizing that public cloud alone does not always meet their non-functional requirements.

According to Fortune, HPE has already sold some of their products to financial services companies. Even the specific date to launce the new product is not settled yet, but HPE said it would come in early 2018. Besides, more clients could gain access to testing environment later this year.

HPE was founded after HP splited into two companies in November 2015. HPE (Hewlett-Packard Enterprise) is mainly for cloud-based computing solutions, while the other one HP Inc, mainly operates PC and Printer business.
