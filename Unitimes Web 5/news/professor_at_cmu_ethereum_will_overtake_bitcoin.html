Professor at CMU Predicts Ethereum Will Overtake Bitcoin as The Number One Cryptocurrency

“According to Professor Vipul Goyal, Ethereum’s main edge over Bitcoin is its powerful and versatile blockchain platform that goes beyond the facilitation of payments”

December 02, 2017 – Vipul Goyal, a professor at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) who teaches graduate courses on blockchain and cryptocurrencies, predicts that Ethereum will overtake Bitcoin as the top cryptocurrency. In an interview with howstuffworks, he added that the drastic increase in new ICOs will soon stabilize, resulting in only “a few dozen in circulation”.

"My personal prediction is that Ethereum will end up becoming the biggest cryptocurrency," Goyal said.

Goyal said that Ethereum is more than a cryptocurrency, as it is primarily a platform enabling various applications to run on blockchain securely. This sets it apart from Bitcoin, whose only objective is to replace traditional currency. Ethereum’s blockchain infrastructure is therefore said to be more “powerful and versatile”, allowing for a wide array of use cases (apart from currency and payments) on its platform.

Goyal also said that individuals, developers and startups are finding it easy to create decentralized apps (Dapps) on the Ethereum network, as Ethereum’s programming language is easy to adopt. One example of this pioneering programming language is the idea of a “smart contract”, where a deal between two parties can be programmed on Ethereum’s platform. These contracts enforce themselves automatically, and can “even impose penalties for a breach”.

Another example of a use case on Ethereum’s blockchain platform is the idea of “programmable money”, illustrated by Andrew Miller, assistant professor of electrical and computer engineering at the University of Illinois, in the same interview as Goyal.

“Programmable money” allows a party to limit transactions once they hit a pre-set ceiling. From Miller:

“A student can withdraw up to $100 a week for expenses, but anything beyond that requires an authorization by the parents' unique cryptography key.”

Miller also thinks that programmable money is one of those ideas that will quickly spread into mainstream banking.
