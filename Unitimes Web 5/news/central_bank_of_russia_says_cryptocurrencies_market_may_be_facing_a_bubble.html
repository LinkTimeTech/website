Central Bank of Russia Says Cryptocurrencies Market May Be Facing A “Bubble”

"According to the regulator’s Financial Stability Report, Russia will also develop a regulatory framework to protect consumers and prevent illegal activities funded by cryptocurrencies "

The Central Bank of Russia has cautioned that the cryptocurrencies market is a “bubble” that may lead to many consumers facing heavy losses in the future – according to Russian news agency TASS. These remarks were made in the regulator’s Financial Stability Report released on Tuesday (28 Nov).

The report also highlighted that users may transact in cryptocurrencies when laundering money and financing terrorism. Accordingly, Russia will develop a regulatory framework to protect consumers and prevent the use of virtual currencies for illegal purposes.

"A ‘bubble’ on the cryptocurrencies market may result in substantial loses for consumers. Also, there are risks that cryptocurrencies are used for money laundering and financing terrorism. The task of national and supranational regulators is to minimize those risks via developing a coordinated approach to regulation of the cryptocurrencies market and restricting the potential of high-risk investments and transactions," the report said.

Other cryptocurrency developments in Russia this week

Just last week, Nikolai Nikiforov, Russian Minister of Communications and Mass Media commented that Russia is unlikely to legalize the use of bitcoin in the country, as bitcoin is a foreign currency. He however went on to say that the Russian government is currently looking at different use cases for blockchain technology.

"Bitcoin is a foreign project for using blockchain technology, the Russian law will never consider bitcoin as a legal entity in the jurisdiction of the Russian Federation. However, I think that it is quite possible to use blockchain technology and the use of various digital tokens," Nikiforov said.
