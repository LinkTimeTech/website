San Francisco Federal Reserve Bank President Says No to Native Digital Currency, While Counterpart in New York Says Yes

“Remarks by John Williams also reaffirm confidence in blockchain technology, in the U.S.”

December 01, 2017 – The President of the Federal Reserve Bank in San Francisco, U.S. John Williams remarked that they are not looking to develop a native cryptocurrency to facilitate faster transactions. However, he said that the U.S. Central Bank is researching blockchain technology enthusiastically for possible applications and use cases. His remarks were made at an economic outlook luncheon in Phoenix.

“Right now, the Federal Reserve is not developing its own digital currency,” Williams said. “Central banks worldwide are interested in how the technology that underlies bitcoin could make payments safer, cheaper and more efficient. Some may also consider adopting digital currencies as official money,” he added.

His comments deviate slightly from remarks made by his counterpart in New York William Dudley, who is the President of the Federal Reserve Bank there. Just a day ago (Nov 29), Dudley said that they are just starting to consider developing their native cryptocurrency.

"I think at this point it’s really very premature to be talking about the Federal Reserve offering digital currencies, but it is something we are starting to think about," Dudley said.
