Forbes: 5 Predictions On How Blockchain Will Drive Digital Transformation

The technology that once was the lesser known force behind Bitcoin is now seeing its own day in the sun. There will be a time when it seems impossible that we ever survived without blockchain technology.

Recently, Daniel Newman, the principal analyst of Futurum Research and CEO of Broadsuite Media Group, expressed his five predictions on how blockchain will drive digital transformation. Here is a chance to give a glimpse of the details:

1. Banks? You may no longer see them！

Because of banks, the good old days when you hide your money beneath the mattress have gone. And because of blockchain, one day in the future you may miss banks. Due to the “middle man”, we have to pay exorbitant transaction service fees in financial transactions. The blockchain technology can essentially solve this problem.

In simple terms, if all currencies are turned digital, there is no need to store or secure them any more. In addition to Bitcoin, blockchain could be used to develop local currency or internationally accepted money. Of course, it depends on the needs of the industry and users.

2. Secure Data? Absolutely Guaranteed!

In the age of the internet of things, information security is a top issue on everyone’s mind. With blockchain, this concern will no longer be in existence. The integrity of the data is guaranteed, thereby it’s impossible to destroy data without being detected.

3. Exchange Fee? Time to Farewell to It！

Have you ever come across a circumstance like this: you are trying to remit money to a foreign country, by the time you figure out the transfer fees, you realize that the amount that recipients are about to get is far less than the original that you are going to remit. Such is the current situation of currency exchange and international remittance.

Blockchain and its promising “borderless crytocurrencies” could make it easier to do foreign trade. It is easier and faster in doing transaction without intermediaries. What’s more, it is more convenient for immigrants to remit the money to their home-based families. At the same time, this is also what digital transformation is all about---helping users live better and keep the money they earn secure.

4. Tracking Products? From Its Origin to Store-shelf!

Imagine you are going to purchase a pair of jeans, if the conditions is that merchants could provide you a clear clue of which factory the cotton came from, the quality of the cotton when harvested as well as the picking time of the cotton, you would absolutely get a more precise evaluation of the product quality, while blockchain can make that happen.

As a distributed ledger in a decentralized fashion, blockchain can record every step of the commodity transactions. In this way, merchants will be able to pinpoint an item’s history — and quality — all the way back to its form as raw materials so as to better evaluate the quality of supplier’s products, besides, the chance of vendors altering history information to avoid or penalty for inferior items.

5. Cloud Storage? Safer! Easier! Faster!

If you’re blossoming, the breeze will come your way. Given the huge power of blockchain, it’s no surprise that various tech giants including Microsoft, Amazon, SAP and IBM would catch up by means of introducing cloud service projects based on blockchain. In the coming years, a safer, easier and faster way to save your data — BaaS is sure to be known by people, and makes our future life more interesting!
