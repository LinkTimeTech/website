The EU Has Invested over €5 Million in Blockchain Startups

“The EU explores Blockchain technology, investing big amount of money to help startups in this space.”

The European Union has invested more than €5 million (about $6 million) in startups covering Blockchain based projects.

So far, six startups developing or researching applications of the technology have received funding through the Horizon 2020 innovation initiative, according to the data.

The work of the three Startups, Signaturit, Authenteq and the Billon Group, have been elaborated last week, that is developing services about digital identity and payments among other use cases.

According to the Horizon 2020 data, EU has invested €5,471,131 to the group of startups. Among which, three startups received more than €1 million apiece, while three others received €50,000 each. Last month, the European Commission said that it was planning to create a "Blockchains for Social Good" competition that would link with the Horizon 2020.

Besides, European developers are exploring other projects to fund the cryptocurrency related projects.
