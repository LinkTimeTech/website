UBS Applies for Intellectual Property Protection for Smart Contract Blockchain Validation

“The largest financial services company in Europe-United Bank of Switzerland is looking to creating more secure computer systems for the patent application of smart contract blockchain validation.”

The U.S. Patent and Trademark Office released a patent application made by United Bank of Switzerland (UBS) last Thursday. In this application, UBS explains how it uses a distributed ledger to create a permanent record of every action on the chain for transaction validation.

The key advantages a distributed ledger has compared to traditional technologies are the decentralization and immutability. This application program will add a record on the blockchain when it uses a smart contract to automatically register. Once the record is added, the user will automatically receive a notification sent by the same contract.

As UBS explains, the application can put the smart contract on the same blockchain that stores the data added and develop two separate blockchains: one is to store data; and the other is to store the data added for smart contract monitoring.

The document emphasizes, “Although a number of non-blockchain-based technologies related to account and data security, authentication, and verification exist (e.g, authorization and access to financial or other accounts, verification of buyers of purchase transactions, verification of owners of securities, verification of notaries of documents, etc.), many of these technologies rely on the trust of the third party managing the database and lack one or more advantages of blockchain technology.”

According to Sergio Ermotti, CEO of UBS, the company has long been interested in blockchain applications and it believes that blockchain technology plays a decisive role in changing and reshaping its industry.

It is known that the company had launched a blockchain-based trade transaction platform in cooperation with IBM at the end of 2016, called Batavia. In early October 2017, the project has been expanding with four major banks (Bank of Montreal ,CaixaBank;Commerzbank and Erste Group) joining it.
