According to Preiss, the global economy is getting worse as the burden of debt is increasing day by day; and the financial system worldwide is based on debt, which has caused quantitative easing and “too much debt and too little growth.” Preiss explains that bitcoin has enormous potential that could drive the fourth industrial revolution.

“Governments have been facing many issues with debt repayment over time, and the whole world has suffered a lot from debt problems. Maybe now the world needs another alternative- bitcoin, an asset-backed currency with limited supply”, said Pressi, “Bitcoin is more than an investment vehicle because it has the attributes that can create another alternative financial system for the society.”

When Pressi was asked about individuals and organizations using bitcoin for such illegal activities as ransomware, he said that the concerns were quite reasonable. However, compared with the U.S. dollar used for illegal activities, the illegal activities involving bitcoin are negligible. Moreover, Pressi pointed out that the USD used for criminal transactions is much more than bitcoin, and in comparison to bitcoin’s fully traceable public blockchain, it is hard to trace any USD transactions.

“The question that always comes is: Is it a currency?”, Pressi said. As he explains, that’s maybe too limiting -bitcoin is much more than a currency; it’s actually a token; it’s distributed trust and distributed consensus; trust between people is less and less; the social contract is beginning to fail, and a decentralized financial infrastructure is probably the trend of further development. “In fact, bitcoin has got a firm foothold within traditional monetary systems.” Pressi said, “Bitcoin’s market capitalization overtook Deutsche Bank last week, which tells us that the world is changing.”
