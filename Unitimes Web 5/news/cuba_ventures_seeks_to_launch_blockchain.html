Cuba Ventures seeks to launch blockchain-enabled Revolupay® app and Cryptocurrency ₡CU Coin for remittance and tourism industry

“Mobile app will facilitate remittances in Cuba, and ₡CU Coin will be used as a centralized currency for payments in the tourism sector”

Cuba Ventures Corp. announced that it will develop Revolupay® app, a blockchain-enabled mobile app to facilitate the S$3 billion remittance industry in Cuba. The Canadian-based company will also introduce cryptocurrency ₡CU Coin as a centralized currency for all visitors around the globe to pay for tourism services in Cuba.

Revolupay®

According to the fact sheet published by Cuba Ventures, remittance companies in Cuba presently charge between 14% - 25% per transaction, and take a long time to complete remittances due to the use of outdated payment processes.

Revolupay® aims to provide remittances at a cheaper and faster rate, with Cuba Ventures claiming that commissions will be lowered by over 50% and remittances can be conducted between users immediately and seamlessly.

Revolupay® will also be used to facilitate payments in Cuba, as it seeks private companies and entrepreneurs to be part of its user base. According to the factsheet: “Revolupay® will allow tourists, intending to visit Cuba, to add funds to their Revolupay® accounts for instant payments to Cuban private businesses.”

Furthermore, Revolupay® seeks to provide another channel for app users to withdraw physical money. Accordingly, the app “allows users to withdraw cash at Revolupay® approved Cuban banks.”

₡CU Coin

Developed in conjunction with Revolupay® and the CubaFIN lending platform, ₡CU Coin will peg its exchange rate against major currencies, which enables tourists to exchange for ₡CU Coin to be used in Cuba. Although ₡CU Coin is slated to rollout first in the Bahamas and Cuba, Cuba Ventures has big plans for ₡CU Coin as a globally accepted cryptocurrency.

“Most visitors to the Caribbean emanate from industrialized high GDP nations. Targeting the Caribbean initiates an interesting cycle of adopters, eventually resulting in world acceptance.” According to Cuba Ventures.
