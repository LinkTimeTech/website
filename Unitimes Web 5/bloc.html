Russia’s Finance Ministry drafts law to Cryptocurrencies

Editor: Xue Xu

11th Sept, 2017

According to the Russia’s Finance Minister, Anton Siluanov, the regulators are preparing the draft bill on legalization of cryptocurrencies in the Russian Federation. The government of Russia will likely make bitcoin and other digital currencies legal in the country.

Russia’s view on digital currency seems to be changing. Only a year ago, the authorities threatened bitcoin users with up to seven years in jail for selling, mining and issuing the digital currency.

The Finance minister suggests that buying digital currencies can be a way like buying financial loan bonds. Siluanov said investors may face a high risk in trading digital currencies because the lacking of security and liquidity.

“The state understands indeed that crypto-currencies are real. There is no sense in banning them, there is a need to regulate them,” Siluanov said that during the financial forum in Moscow.

Besides, the country is open to the adoption and the development of blockchain. Vladimir Putin, the President of Russia, showed his interest in this technology during the meeting with Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin this summer. The conversation took place during International Economic Forum, where Buterin discussed the possible applications of blockchain technology and its potential to create the new digital economy.
