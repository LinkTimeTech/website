Shanghai 17 Platforms Stop Virtual Currency Trading and the Top Management Team Is Asked to Stay

Editor: Xue Xu

23th Sept. 2017

On Sept. 4th, Seven government administrations including the People’s Bank of China, China Securities Regulatory Commission, China Banking Regulatory Commission and China Insurance Regulatory Commission have all come together to issue a joint statement, echoing the committee’s stance on how ICOs are a form of unauthorized illegal fund raising, which had brought a devastating impact on major trading platforms in China.

According to the statement, all organizations and individuals are now prohibited from the raising funds through any ICO activities. Those that have already done so though, should make the arrangements necessary to return the funds they raised in order to protect their investors. In Shanghai, more than 90% of the ICOs projects that had raised funds via ICOs also have to liquidate the proceeds and pay refunds to investors.

According to the report, the Shanghai government at both district and municipal level, Shanghai-based PBOC (People’s Bank of China) and other Regulatory authorities have taken proactive measures. They conducted thorough investigation, and talked to the top management of these platforms, specifying supervisory requirements. All cryptocurrency exchange platforms must stop trading by Sept. 30, 2017. At present, initial progress has been made in this regard. 17 trading platforms involved in ICOs and bitcoin trading such as BTCChina, Binance, Lhang, Bitekuang, ICOAGE, 91ICO, ICOrace, ICORace and ICO17, etc. have publicly announced that they will stop before the deadline (four of them have stopped trading already). BTCChina, one of China’s largest bitcoin trading platforms based in Shanghai announced on September 14, 2017 that it would shut down the registration channel from the date of the announcement, and will stop all trading from September 30, 2017.

According to the report, following the halting of the trading on September 30, 2017, the Shanghai government would supervise and urge all trading platforms to ensure that investors could get their funds back. According to the Shanghai Internet Financial Risks Remediation, like Beijing, the top management of these platforms is asked to stay in Shanghai to cooperate with the refunding activities.
